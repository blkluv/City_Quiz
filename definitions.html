Capital cities

Normally the capital city of a country is the de jure government capital of the country, with some exceptions as described below.

Country does not have a de jure capital city

Switzerland: the federal city, Bern is considered the capital.
Nauru: the de facto capital, Yaren, where government institutions are located, is considered the capital.

Country has multiple de jure capitals

This only applies to South Africa, where all three capitals, Cape Town (legislative), Pretoria (executive) and Bloemfontein (judical) are included in the game.

A de facto capital exists in addition to the de jure capital

Benin: de jure capital, Porto-Novo, is used.
Bolivia: de facto capital, La Paz, is used.
Burundi: de jure capital, Gitega, is used.
Côte d’Ivoire: de jure capital, Yamousoukro, is used.
Eswatini: administrative capital, Eswatini, is used.
Honduras: de facto capital and part of de jure capital district, Tegucigalpa, is used.
Malaysia: de jure capital, Kuala Lumpur, is used.
Montenegro: de jure capital, Podgorica, is used.
The Netherlands: de jure capital, Amsterdam, is used.
Sri Lanka: de jure capital, Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte, is used.
Tanzania: de jure capital, Dodoma, is used.

Size rankings

The city size rankings in the game are based off various online sources. These might have numbers from different times, or different definitions for where to count population. This might cause some cities to be on one list and not another, even though if the same population count was used, it would have qualified.

The lists in the game is based off the following sources.
